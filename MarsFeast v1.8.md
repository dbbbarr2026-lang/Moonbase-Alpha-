### Clover Dome Addition (New Symbiotic Structure Integrated with Legs 1 & 3)
A pressurized **Clover Dome** (inflatable or rigid dome with clover ground cover for nitrogen fixation, soil stabilization, and pleasant micro-environment) is established adjacent to or integrated with the tipped/buried Starship complex via PRUFROCK tunnels. In 0.38g, dome construction benefits from reduced structural loads and easier regolith anchoring.  

Inside the Clover Dome:  
- **Laying hens**: Small, efficient flock providing fresh eggs (high-quality protein, fats, vitamins) fed primarily from superfood and crop residues.  
- **Bees**: Honeybee colonies for pollination of greenhouse crops (boosting yields of tomatoes, peas, moringa, etc.) and production of honey (natural sweetener, antioxidants, morale booster).  
- **Clover cover**: Nitrogen-fixing ground cover that supports forage and enhances the dome’s bioregenerative atmosphere.  

**Ammonia Scrubber Integration**  
Chicken manure and respiration produce ammonia (NH₃). A dedicated **ammonia scrubber** (bioregenerative or physicochemical) captures NH₃ from the Clover Dome atmosphere with high efficiency. The recovered nitrogen is converted into usable forms (ammonium salts or nitrates via nitrifying bacteria) and **fed directly into other Legs**:  
- Leg 1: Nutrient boost for hydroponics, superfoods, and Lunar Arabica coffee.  
- Leg 3: Accelerates bioremediation and soil organic matter buildup.  

This creates an additional closed-loop nitrogen cycle, improving overall ECLSS integration and reducing Earth dependency. Optimus swarm maintains the scrubber, monitors flock/hive health, and harvests eggs/honey with minimal human intervention. CHMODA oversees animal welfare and nutritional contributions.

### Superfoods Portfolio (Strategic, High-Density Crops in Leg 1)
Within the original Leg 1 greenhouse (hybrid hydroponics + bioremediated regolith soil in tipped/buried Starships) and Leg 3 soil regeneration, the following **strategic superfoods** are cultivated for maximal nutritional return per volume/energy:  
- **Duckweed**: Fastest-growing, highest plant protein (40-45% dry weight, complete amino acids), zeaxanthin/lutein for radiation protection; thrives on digester slurry.  
- **Watercress & Microgreens** (amaranth, quinoa, red cabbage): 2–40× nutrient density (polyphenols, anthocyanins, vitamins); short 7–15 day cycles; stacked trays for vertical efficiency.  
- **Moringa oleifera**: High protein, bioavailable calcium, iron, antioxidants/polyphenols for bone support and oxidative stress.  
- Algae for cream and supplements.  

These complement (without replacing) original crops such as potatoes, peas, and tomatoes. Bees from the Clover Dome provide pollination support for higher yields. All processing remains **rudimentary** only.

**Flatbread**: Red lentil + sweet potato mash flatbread (simple mashing, mixing, and quick cooking on methane griddles using Raptor/ISRU heat). This maintains the communal "Red Planet" ritual intent with minimal processing.

This aroma-rich ritual provides powerful psychological resilience and morale support over the 5-year mission.

**Coffee Power Footprint Justification**  
Lunar Arabica coffee is grown in a compact, dedicated section of Leg 1 using **low-power, spectrum-optimized LEDs** (primarily red ~660 nm and blue ~450 nm wavelengths with far-red supplementation for efficient photosynthesis and bean quality; modern horticultural LEDs achieve ~2.5–3.0 µmol/J).  

The modest allocation is justified because the ritual delivers outsized Crew Experience benefits (aroma, mild stimulant, polyphenols) that combat isolation and menu fatigue far more effectively than equivalent resources spent on generic calories. It is fully supported by Raptor ISRU baseload during global dust storms. CHMODA monitors caffeine to balance with bone health. Net benefit to long-term mission success outweighs the small dedicated power/volume footprint.
